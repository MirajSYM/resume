This is the capstone project from Google Advanced Data Analytics Professional Program on Coursera



Stakeholder

Stakeholders for this project include the HR department, management team, and company executives who are interested in understanding employee turnover and identifying factors contributing to attrition.

Goal

The goal is to analyze employee turnover and identify key factors influencing attrition, enabling the company to take proactive measures for employee retention and improve workforce management.


Explore the Project notebook here.


Objective

The objective of this project was to analyze a comprehensive HR dataset and build predictive models to gain insights into why employees leave the company. By understanding the key drivers of attrition, we aimed to provide actionable recommendations to reduce turnover and enhance employee retention.



Approach

We adopted the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to guide our analysis:

1. Situation:

Our dataset comprised various employee attributes, such as satisfaction level, last evaluation, number of projects, average monthly hours, tenure, work accidents, promotions, salary, and department. It included both employees who had left the company and those who had stayed.

2. Task:

The primary task was to conduct Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to understand the relationships between variables and identify patterns and trends. We also wanted to explore the impact of different factors on employee attrition and create predictive models to make informed decisions.

3. Action:

During EDA, we discovered that the dataset contained duplicates, which we promptly removed. We renamed columns and encoded categorical variables to make them suitable for model building. We visualized the data using various plots, such as box plots, scatter plots, and bar charts, to uncover insights.

Next, we applied machine learning models, including Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost, to predict employee attrition. We optimized hyperparameters using GridSearchCV and evaluated the models using multiple metrics, including accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and AUC-ROC. And the results are:

Comparison of Evaluation Metrics:

Logistic Regression:
  • Accuracy: 82.51%
  • Precision: 46.69%
  • Recall (Sensitivity or True Positive Rate): 26.19%
  • F1-score: 33.56%
  • AUC-ROC: 88.13%
Random Forest Classifier:
  • Accuracy: 98.50%
  • Precision: 98.05%
  • Recall: 92.80%
  • F1-score: 95.35%
  • AUC-ROC: 98.06%

4. Result

Our analysis revealed several key insights:

  • Employees with low satisfaction levels and low tenure were more likely to leave the company.
  • Work accidents did not appear to have a significant impact on attrition.
  • Lack of promotions was associated with higher attrition rates.
  • Departments sales and technical departments experienced higher attrition.
  • Salary levels were also a crucial factor in influencing attrition rates, with employees in the low salary bracket being more likely to leave.
