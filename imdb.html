Results:

Key findings from the analysis include:

  • Most movies have a rating between 8.0 and 9.0, indicating strong audience reception.
  • 1995 had the highest number of top movies, followed by 2004 and 1957.
  • R and PG-13 are common certificate ratings, suggesting a mature audience target.
  • Directors like Christopher Nolan and Akira Kurosawa feature prominently, with versatility contributing to success.
  • Longer movies (180 minutes) are popular, showcasing audience preference for immersive experiences.
  • PG-13 dominates Action, Adventure, and Sci-Fi genres, while R-rated movies are common in Action, Crime, and Drama.
  • Average ratings fluctuate over decades, with peaks in the 1970s and 1990s.

Conclusion:
The analysis provides insights into factors contributing to movie success. Ratings, release years, certificate ratings, and directorial versatility emerge as crucial elements. Filmmakers can use these findings to enhance their craft, while audiences can make informed choices for an enjoyable movie experience.
