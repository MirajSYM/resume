A/B Testing: Unveiling User Engagement & The Impact of Website Themes on Behavior and Beyond

  • Category: Data Science
  • Tech Stack: Python, Pandas, Numpy, Matplotlib, SciPy
  • Concept: Hypothesis Testing, Bonferroni correction

This case study is completed on the dataset by statso.io

Situation:

In the course of conducting exploratory data analysis (EDA), we delved into a robust dataset encompassing user engagement metrics and demographic information. Our exploration involved scrutinizing Click Through Rates, Conversion Rates, Bounce Rates, Scroll Depths, and Ages, utilizing visualizations such as box plots, scatter plots, histograms, and bar charts to reveal patterns and differences associated with various themes.

Task:

The primary task was to investigate the influence of website themes on user engagement metrics. We initiated hypothesis testing, specifically focusing on Click Through Rates, Conversion Rates, and interactions with the shopping cart.

Action:

  • Click Through Rate (CTR): We posed the question: Does the choice of website theme impact the Click Through Rate? Employing an independent t-test, we compared "Dark Theme" and "Light Theme," ultimately rejecting the null hypothesis with a p-value of 0.048, signifying a significant difference in Click Through Rates.
  • Conversion Rate: Continuing our exploration, we questioned whether themes played a role in user sign-ups. Despite a p-value of 0.635, the independent t-test did not allow us to reject the null hypothesis, indicating that themes had a negligible impact on conversions.
  • Added_to_Cart: Turning our attention to cart interactions, we examined whether themes guided users toward adding items to their carts. The chi-squared test with a p-value of 0.717 led us to uphold the null hypothesis, suggesting that themes had little influence on users' cart interactions.

Result:

Recognizing the risk of inflated Type I errors in multiple hypothesis testing, we implemented the Bonferroni correction to safeguard against erroneous conclusions. This correction method ensured that our conclusions maintained steadfastness and reliability.
